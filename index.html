Assia town
It is a town in the Mountains of Batroun located on a high plateau at an altitude (with its outskirts) from 750m to approximately 1100m. It is on an area of 945 hectares, but the area of the village is in reality more than that. It is about 975 hectares, so it is among the first five ranks ranks the largest areas in Batroun District.
The town of Assia is bordered by more than 12 villages, and it is considered the largest area in the Batroun district in terms of the number of villages surrounding it. They are: Tartaj, Hadtoun, Rasha, Alali, Marah Al-Hajj, Chabtin, Zan, Kfarshleiman, Helta, Jebla, Dael, Aura, Qandoula, Bashtodar, Bchaaleh, Nahla, ...

Assia has about 25 neighborhoods and three regions (Yareta, Sharifa, Brareja).

It is an estate famous for making pottery by hands using traditional methods since BC. When the Phoenicians came, they brought with them the wheel that is currently used in pottery making. However, the town of Assia did not use this machine, and it remained in the old traditional method without any kind of development intervening in it, and the industry remained the focus of attention.
